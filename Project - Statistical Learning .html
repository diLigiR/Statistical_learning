skewness = 0 : normally distributed.
skewness > 0 : more weight in the left tail of the distribution.
skewness < 0 : more weight in the right tail of the distribution.

HYPOTHESIS TESTING¶

4. a. Do charges of people who smoke differ significantly from the people who don't?

  • Stating the Hypothesis

Ho : Charges of people who smoke is same as those who don't¶

Ha : Charges of people who smoke and those who don't are different¶

In [115]:
from scipy.stats import ttest_ind
In [116]:
sample1  = data[data["smoker"]=='yes']
sample2 = data[data["smoker"]=='no']
In [122]:
#statistical significance level 0.05
# two-sample t-test
# null hypothesis: the two groups have the same mean
# this test assumes the two groups variance is not equal( Welch's T-Test)
t_statistic, p_value = ttest_ind(sample1["charges"], sample2["charges"],equal_var=False)
print(t_statistic, p_value)

32.751887766341824 5.88946444671698e-103
In [123]:
# p_value < 0.05 => alternative hypothesis:
# they don't have the same mean at the 5% significance level
print ("two-sample t-test p-value=", p_value)

two-sample t-test p-value= 5.88946444671698e-103

Since p-value is very low below the significance level , we can reject the null hypothesis that the charges are same for smoker's and non-smokers¶

The boxplots show almost dissimilar shapes, location and spreads and there are a few ouliers.¶

Step 1: State the null and alternative hypothesis:¶

  • $H_0$: $\mu1$ = $\mu2$ = $\mu3$
  • $H_A$: At least one $\mu$ differs

Here $\mu1$, $\mu2$ and $\mu3$ are the mean of bmi for women with 0 , 1 and 2 children respectively

Step 2: Decide the significance level¶

Here we select α = 0.05
